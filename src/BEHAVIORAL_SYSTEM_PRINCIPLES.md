## Design Philosophy: Calm Mastery

### Visual Principles:
- **Calm teal background** - Reduces anxiety
- **Purple/pink rewards** - Celebration without overstimulation
- **No red for failure** - No shame, ever
- **XP never decreases** - Progress is always forward
- **Clean, simple layouts** - Reduces cognitive load

### Tone Principles:
- **Containment over chaos** - Predictability calms ADHD brains
- **Effort over perfection** - Rewards trying, not getting it right
- **Visible over abstract** - Parents see proof, not promises
- **Reassurance over urgency** - Belonging, not pressure

---

## The Weekly Rhythm (Containment Is Key)

**Why it works:** Predictability calms ADHD brains and reduces household chaos

### Non-Negotiable Schedule:
- **Monday:** Roblox Math
- **Tuesday:** Reading
- **Wednesday:** Writing
- **Thursday:** STEAM / Projects
- **Friday:** Executive Functioning

**Benefits:**
- Students anticipate the week
- Parents stop asking "what's today?"
- ADHD brains feel safe inside routine
- Reduces decision fatigue

### Completion Window:
- Live or recording allowed
- Challenges due by Friday
- **No late penalties** - only "needs attention" (never "overdue")

---

## The Three-Step Structure (Cognitive Load Reduction)

**Psychology:** Stop the parent's brain from spinning

### Step 1: One Predictable Weekly Rhythm
- Same days
- Same subjects
- No guessing

### Step 2: Quests + Challenges
- Each subject is a quest
- Each quest has clear challenges
- XP and badges reward effort

### Step 3: Visible Progress
- Parents see weekly summaries
- Students see completion, not comparison
- Mastery is clear

**Why this works:** Replaces chaos with containment (critical for ADHD households)
